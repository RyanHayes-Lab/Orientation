# Other Group Information

## Conferences to consider attending

ACS - The American Chemical Society holds spring and fall meetings. These are very large conferences of around 10,000 chemists, so it’s very easy to get lost, but they almost always have interesting talks going if you can find them.

AIChE - The chemical engineering society has a similar meeting annually in November; I haven’t attended yet, so I can’t tell if it’s useful

Biophysical society - This is a medium size conference, maybe a couple thousand, usually in February, the topics are quite diverse, but I learned a lot about biophysics the first time I went here. It covers everything from molecular dynamics to structural determination to lipid rafts and liquid-liquid phase separation.

Protein society - This is a medium size conference of about 1200 scientists focused on proteins, everything from simulation to design to aggregation to folding, characterization, intrinsically disordered proteins and more. There are typically two sessions running at a time to choose between, and it runs at the end of June / beginning of July.

Gordon Research Conference on Protein Folding and Dynamics - There are many Gordon Research Conferences for different topics, held every other year with an attendance limit of 150 participants. They are very focussed on a narrow field, and this has been one of my favorite conferences since I first attended in 2010.

Gordon Research Conference on Computer Aided Drug Design - This is a conference Mobley lab members often attend. They cover biologics like antibodies, but it is an especially good conference to attend if you work on small molecules
[https://www.grc.org/computer-aided-drug-design-conference/2027/](https://www.grc.org/computer-aided-drug-design-conference/2027/)

Gordon Research Conference on Protein Engineering - I have heard good things about this conference from Jennifer Prescher. Someone from the group should check it out. It is held July in odd years.  
[https://www.grc.org/protein-engineering-conference/](https://www.grc.org/protein-engineering-conference/)

Gordon Research Conference on Computational Chemistry - Sometimes Mobley lab members attend this GRC. It is held even years in July.  
[https://www.grc.org/computational-chemistry-conference/](https://www.grc.org/computational-chemistry-conference/)

Free Energy Meeting - This conference occurs May of every other year and is focussed on computing free energies more accurately for big pharma applications in industry. Consequently, about half of the attendees are from industry. It’s capped around 200. On the off years they are trying to organize a similar conference in Europe.  
[http://alchemistry.org/wiki/Events](http://alchemistry.org/wiki/Events)

Antibody Engineering and Therapeutics Conference - Specialized conference on antibody engineering. Amirmasoud has attended in the past, is more industrial and experimental, will give a broad overview of the antibody engineering field.  
[https://informaconnect.com/antibody-engineering-therapeutics/](https://informaconnect.com/antibody-engineering-therapeutics/)

RosettaCON - RosettaCON is a protein design conference centered around the Rosetta community. The summer conference is early August near University of Washington, and Winter RosettaCON is typically on the east coast. This is a great place to meet other computational protein design scientists.

## Journals to consider for publication

Journal of Chemical Theory and Computation (JCTC) - This is the preeminent journal in our field for method development papers. QC from the CHARMM community is an editor here.

Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling (JCIM) - This is the preeminent journal in our field from applications and benchmarks of molecular simulations. They also accept method development studies.

Journal of Computational Chemistry (JCC) - JCC is another high impact journal in our field. Charlie Brooks was recently an editor here, and Jana Shen who does constant pH simulations has replaced him.

For higher impact work beyond our local field of molecular simulation, JACS and PNAS are good options for publication. For work aimed at the broader community of protein scientists, Proteins may be appropriate. For work that is intended to be updated, the Living Journal of Computatioal Molecular Science (LiveCoMS) may be appropriate.

## Other groups to join

Please join the Synthetic and Chemical Biology Club (SCBC) Google Group. The club host a 1 hour seminar once a month with two trainee speakers, and the meetings are announced through the google group.
[https://sites.uci.edu/scbc/](https://sites.uci.edu/scbc/)

To join:

- while logged in to Google with your UCI account, go to groups.google.com
- in the left pane, choose “all groups”
- search for “scbc” using the search function at the top
- click on “–>]” to join

Please contact [dsoenen@uci.edu](dsoenen@uci.edu) if you have any problems.
